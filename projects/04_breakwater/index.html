Submerged breakwater hydrodynamics

Flume studies of porous vs. solid submerged breakwaters and their impact on river hydraulics.

My earliest research, as an undergraduate at the Department of Water Resources Engineering, BUET (advisor: Ataur Rahman), used physical flume modeling to compare the hydraulic performance of porous and solid submerged breakwaters.

In a 21.3 m wave flume, we tested horizontally slotted breakwaters at three porosities against a solid rectangular breakwater under matched wave conditions, measuring transmission, reflection, and wave-energy-loss coefficients across breakwater width and porosity. Porous breakwaters transmitted less wave energy per unit material than expected but reduced wave height less than the solid design — a trade-off relevant to designing low-cost, permeable coastal and riverbank protection.

That work extended into a field-based study of the Feni-Muhuri river system, where flood-frequency analysis, tidal records, and a coupled HEC-RAS 1D/HEC-RAS 2D-Delft3D model were used to evaluate how bridge piers, abutments, and other hydraulic structures reshape planform, thalweg position, and scour.

Published in Procedia Engineering (2017) and the Journal of Water Resources and Pollution Studies (2017).
