With **Subir Biswas**, **M. Shahjahan Mondal**, **Binata Roy**, and **Afeefa Rahman** at the Institute of Water and Flood Management, BUET, I assessed flood risk in the Arial Khan River floodplain in south-central Bangladesh from an integrated perspective — combining physical hazard with socioeconomic exposure and vulnerability, all changing over time rather than held constant.

Flood hazard was simulated with a two-dimensional hydrodynamic model, while exposure and vulnerability were projected using statistical techniques, and principal component analysis was used to weight indicators of hazard, exposure, sensitivity, and adaptive capacity. Flood depth, duration, and extent are projected to increase from baseline to the 2080s under RCP 2.6 and RCP 8.5 scenarios, even as improving adaptive capacity lowers sensitivity and vulnerability — with low-risk area projected to grow from 62% at baseline to 85–91% by the 2080s.

This built on a calibrated HEC-HMS hydrologic model coupled with a HEC-RAS 1D/2D hydraulic model for the same floodplain, validated against observed hydrologic data and spatial flood evidence to produce applied flood-risk maps for water-resources planning.

Published in <a href="https://doi.org/10.1111/jfr3.12876">Journal of Flood Risk Management (2023)</a>.
